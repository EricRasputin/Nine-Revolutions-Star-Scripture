# Chapter 17: Wang Lang's Dominance

"Father!" Watching her father's leaping figure recede, Li Shanshan's eyes had long since filled with tears, and her right hand reached out as if to catch hold of his retreating back.

"My Lady, please come away with this lowly general[^1], and don't let the City Lord's last wish be in vain." General Yang's face was sorrowful too, but he was only a general, and he dared not disobey his lord's command.

Li Shanshan still stood frozen in place. Reluctant as she was, in her heart she knew there was no choice now but to leave.

But just as General Yang was about to lead her away, in the depths of the night Li Shanshan caught sight of a hidden figure that moved at terrible speed, vanishing into the dark in a single instant.

Even so, Li Shanshan had felt a deeply familiar aura.

That aura was far away, yet somehow always close...

It set Li Shanshan thinking. *Such a familiar feeling... could that figure be someone I know?*

Seeing Li Shanshan still standing dazed, General Yang took her by the arm. "My Lady, no more hesitating. While the Wang Family hasn't noticed us yet, let's get out of the city now."

With that, paying no mind to her bewildered face, he led his band of riders and pulled her along, heading for the edge of the city.

Just as General Yang was about to clear the city, little did he know that an ambush was already lying in wait for him.

"Heh-heh, General Yang, where do you think you're going?"

Three figures flashed out of the darkness and blocked General Yang's path.

Once he could make out the three faces, General Yang's expression changed. "It's you! The Three Brothers of Black Wind."

.....

Inside the Su Manor, the battle had grown brutal.

Su Qiang and Wang Zixiang were evenly matched, both wounded, with no clear winner emerging. Su Mo and Su Chen faced equally formidable opponents, the four trading blow for blow, every one of them fighting drenched in blood!

As for Su Beilou, he too was now exchanging blows with Wang Lang.

And one glance was enough to see that the gap between them was all too plain.

"Su Beilou, is this all you've got? It seems this patriarch overestimated you, and I'd advise you to lie down and die quietly, before there's nothing left of you to bury." From start to finish, Wang Lang had not thrown a single move, only dodging Su Beilou's offensive.

The latter was furious in that moment, and equally despairing. He knew the gap between himself and Wang Lang: he stood only at rank-three Divine Martial Realm, while Wang Lang had long since broken through to rank-five.

Just two minor ranks apart, and yet the gap between them was a chasm.

"Save your words. A scholar may be killed but not humiliated[^2], Wang Lang! No matter what happens today, this old man will not let you walk away whole."

"Hyah! High-grade technique, Three Yang Blazing Fire Palm!"

Su Beilou roared in fury, his presence erupting outward, spiritual energy gathering on both palms and lifting bursts of raging fire from them.

Tri-Profound qi spread from across his body onto his palms, and the strength of the flames surged once more.

"Oh? It seems you've finally decided to fight in earnest, since you've even brought out your Su Family's unique art. Very well, in light of the friendship we once shared, I'll grant you a quick death."

"High-grade technique, Astral Wild Bull Force." Against Su Beilou's full-force attack, even Wang Lang, for all his arrogance, did not dare grow careless now.

After all, Su Beilou was a Divine Martial Realm expert. If he came at Wang Lang with no thought for his own life, even Wang Lang couldn't withstand it.

In that instant, Su Beilou's palms unleashed terrible flames in the void, a powerful aura wrapping around him as he launched a frenzied assault on Wang Lang.

Every fist he hammered out, every palm he drove forward, carried the violent force of fire.

One slip and Wang Lang would be ash.

Wang Lang, on the other hand, was already above Su Beilou in cultivation, and the high-grade technique he was now using outmatched Su Beilou's by a wide margin in both presence and raw power.

Astral wind surged, taking the form of a charging wild bull.

No matter how Su Beilou pressed his attack, he could not so much as scratch Wang Lang.

"Su Beilou, you cannot touch me. Keep struggling like this and it will only be in vain." Wang Lang threw back his head and laughed.

From the dark, Su Yang and the Purple Lightning Mad Lion watched it all unfold, and when Su Yang spotted Wang Lang and Wang Yiyi watching the fight from a distance, his fury nearly stole his reason. He was about to move when the Great Sage's voice stopped him in time. "Kid, don't be hasty, it's a dog-eat-dog show, what are you so worked up about? The Su Family's nothing to you now. Your parents aren't even here, so better to wait and see how it plays out, and besides, that old woman's cultivation is no ordinary thing, at least a step above this old Wang. Show yourself now and you might be a sheep walking into a tiger's mouth!"

At those words, Su Yang's furious mind cooled at once.

Indeed, Su Yang had also noticed the old woman standing beside Wang Yiyi. She looked ordinary, yet the spiritual energy raging around the battle did not so much as ripple her. She stood as steady as a mountain.

And Wang Yiyi seemed deeply deferential to the old woman, dropping her usual airs and watching everything in silence.

"Master, that old woman's cultivation is strong. Even I can sense a thread of danger from her." The Purple Lightning Mad Lion said it with a grave look.

"Mm. I understand." Su Yang answered briefly, then fell silent.

What Su Yang couldn't make sense of was why the Wang Family had moved on the Su Family at this moment. Could it really be just because Wang Yiyi had broken through to the Divine Martial Realm? If that was the whole of it, Wang Lang's ambition had grown out of all proportion.

Su Beilou had been the Su Family's Grand Elder, after all, and had treated Su Yang well in the past. But Su Yang had never imagined that, with him gone, Su Beilou would become the Su Family's patriarch.

His own parents, meanwhile, had vanished without a trace...

It set Su Yang on edge. He had to know whether his parents were still alive...

But Su Yang knew well enough that he couldn't afford a rash move right now. If he got pinned down, shaking the Wang Family's pursuit afterward would be no small problem.

Best to wait a while longer.

Su Yang watched the Su Family's battle, and inside him not a ripple stirred.

......

Watching his every offensive turned aside by Wang Lang with ease, Su Beilou felt the panic rising, and the more frantic he grew, the easier the openings came. By this point, all Su Beilou wanted was to fight Wang Lang to the death.

Wang Lang kept up the mockery the whole time. "Brother Su, your technique's too weak, can this really be all the Three Yang Blazing Fire Palm has to give? It seems I did overestimate your Su Family after all. No, more precisely, I overestimated *you*, after Su Lie left. Back when Su Lie and Lin Wanwan were here, this patriarch wouldn't have dared even think of moving against your Su Family."

"Pity, though. After that damned bastard vanished, who'd have thought you'd turn on them so heartlessly."

"Su Beilou, you and I could have gotten along well enough. It's just that I have a rule of mine: I don't share my power. So I had no choice but to put a fine pawn like you to use."

"Hahaha!"

In the face of Wang Lang's verbal assault, Su Beilou was already shaking head to toe with rage, his face twisted savagely.

He let out a furious roar. "Wang Lang, I'm taking you with me!"

But Wang Lang had been waiting for Su Beilou to be overcome by rage, and the moment an opening showed, his form blurred. Astral wind gathered on his right fist and erupted outward, the blow slamming into Su Beilou's gut.

"Die!"

A cold smile crept onto Wang Lang's face.

---

[^1]: 末将 (mò jiàng) — literally "the last/lowest general." A formal, self-effacing first-person used by a military officer when addressing a superior. It belongs to the same family of formal third-person self-references already glossed in chapters 3 and 5 (本家主 "this patriarch," 本皇子 "this prince," 老夫 "this old one," 在下 "the one below"), but slots in lower than all of them. General Yang is reminding himself, in his own grammar, that he speaks as a servant. The English "this lowly general" preserves the bow without inventing a synonym for "I."
[^2]: 士可杀不可辱 (shì kě shā bù kě rǔ) — literally "a scholar/gentleman can be killed but not humiliated." A famous saying often traced to the Records of Rites and central to the Confucian conception of the 士 (shì), the gentleman-scholar whose honor outranks his life. Su Beilou is a martial cultivator, not a scholar, but reaching for the saying frames his last stand as a moral one rather than a tactical one. In the Chinese register, that single phrase moves him from "losing fight" to "dying with honor," which is exactly why he says it.
