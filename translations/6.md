# Chapter 6: The Vicious Woman

At that moment, deep in the Demonic Beast Mountain Range.

Deep in the dense jungle, a bare-chested young man gripped a wooden staff and traded blows with a Lightning Leopard, a massive rank-four demonic beast.

Despite its enormous bulk, the Lightning Leopard's size did nothing to slow its strikes. Its razor-sharp claws lashed out like lightning, raking again and again toward the young man before it.

The young man was composed, not the least bit rattled by the Leopard's speed. His staff whirled in his hands, each sweep tearing through the air with a sharp crack, the muscles along his arms rippling with raw power.

Every one of the Lightning Leopard's attacks was deflected with ease.

"ROAR!" The Lightning Leopard's fury reached its breaking point. Fed up with this human's mockery, the Leopard let out a thunderous bellow. Lightning gathered deep in its maw, and in an instant it spat out a powerful bolt, blasting straight toward the young man.

The bare-chested young man was none other than Su Yang, who had spent the past three months in the Demonic Beast Mountain Range.

Su Yang's physical constitution was now worlds apart from what it had once been.

And with the Battle Sage Art flowing through him, he had no fear of taking a hit head-on.

Watching the Lightning Leopard's bolt barrel toward him, Su Yang didn't move an inch. He simply planted his staff in the ground and let the full force of that raging lightning crash into his body.

Zzzt... zzzt... zzzt... zzzt... zzzt...

The lightning swallowed him whole, and thunder cracked through the air.

Su Yang's expression never changed. His jaw was locked tight, his hands trembling without stop, yet his eyes held nothing but iron resolve. No fear. Only the will to press forward.

The Lightning Leopard was beside itself. This human was a mere ordinary cultivator, barely at the True Martial Realm, and yet it was actually standing there absorbing its lightning attacks head-on? The beast went berserk.

And yet this seemingly weak human showed not the slightest fear of its most powerful technique.

It was a rank-four demonic beast with its own territory in this stretch of the Demonic Beast Mountain Range. If the other beasts saw it struggling to put down a measly True Martial Realm cultivator, how would it ever hold its head up again?

How would it ever show off[^3] for the female Lightning Leopards?

So the rank-four beast poured every last drop of its spiritual energy into one devastating assault, channeling the full might of its body into a relentless torrent of lightning and hammering it into Su Yang without pause.

"Tsk, tsk. You're a ruthless one, kid. Deliberately using a Lightning Leopard's own bolts to temper your body. This Great Sage has not misjudged you." The Great Sage's voice resonated in Su Yang's mind.

"Heh. Your methods are brutal enough on their own, Senior. This junior just wanted to have some fun. This Lightning Leopard is no joke, but the lightning really is doing wonders for this junior's body," Su Yang chuckled inwardly.

"Ha! This Great Sage has barely started. What's the matter, can't take it anymore?"

"Tsk."

Su Yang clicked his tongue.

He thought grimly about what these past three months had put him through. It was no easier than three years as a blood slave.

The Battle Sage Art was brutally difficult to cultivate.

It demanded not only the conditioning of the body but the tempering of the soul. For three months Su Yang had lived almost entirely within the black stone, twenty hours of training every day alongside constant attempts at cultivation. The effort had not been wasted. He had reached the True Martial Realm.

Su Yang himself wasn't fully satisfied, but if anyone else knew he had broken into the True Martial Realm in just three months, it would send shockwaves through the city.

He had now grasped the fundamentals of the Battle Sage Art.

In pure physical combat he could hold his ground against a ninth-grade Tri-Profound Realm expert. With his staff, he could match a Tri-Profound Realm cultivator at Perfection.

But fighting a Divine Martial Realm expert was still beyond his reach.

Su Yang understood this clearly. He also knew that Wang Yiyi had broken through to the Divine Martial Realm just yesterday. The knowledge bore down on him like a weight, driving him to use the demonic beasts as tools for tempering his body.

A blood debt must be repaid!!!

That burning conviction blazed in Su Yang's eyes. Spiritual energy surged within him, and the blood of his Supreme Bloodline rushed into the Eight Extraordinary Meridians, flooding every limb and bone.

Golden radiance blazed from every inch of his skin.

An overwhelming aura erupted from him. The Lightning Leopard panicked, immediately cut off its attack, and bolted.

Su Yang had no intention of letting his prey escape. Both fists clenched tight, he transformed into a streak of golden lightning and appeared in front of the Leopard in an instant. The beast skidded to a halt, terror flooding its eyes, and tried to veer off in another direction.

Su Yang's fists were faster than lightning itself. They slammed simultaneously into the Lightning Leopard's skull.

The Leopard's head burst apart like a watermelon, sending blood spraying in every direction.

Su Yang's eyes glinted, cold and predatory as a wild beast, utterly without feeling. He turned and walked slowly toward a small stream nearby.

"With your current strength, you can't defeat a Divine Martial Realm cultivator just yet. Give it another month and you should be there. Keep a cool head, kid." The Great Sage's voice rose again.

Su Yang said nothing. He already knew the truth.

If he went after the Wang Family now, death was the only thing waiting for him. The Wang Family had more than one Divine Martial Realm cultivator. Besides Wang Yiyi, her father Wang Lang had held that realm for years. Revenge wasn't something he could simply take. Yet for these past three months, Su Yang had barely slept. Every time those three years of darkness crept back into his mind, he had to fight the urge to storm the Wang Family's gates that very instant and tear every last one of them apart.

Just as Su Yang had waded into the stream to wash the demonic beast's blood from his body, voices drifted out from the jungle nearby.

In a single fluid motion he sprang from the stream and landed silently in the dense cover of the trees above.

A moment later, several figures emerged near the stream. They were servants of Wang Manor.

Su Yang narrowed his eyes on the group, a killing intent flickering in his gaze.

"Our Wang Family is about to become the top power in Yebei City. The young miss is a true monster of a prodigy[^1], just past fifteen and already at the Divine Martial Realm. It really is something to envy."

"Who's arguing? Word is even the Moongaze Sect Master personally issued an order to take the young miss as her closed-door disciple[^2]."

"I've also heard the Great Xia Dynasty wants the young miss to enter the imperial family. And the one who came to the manor today for exactly that purpose was the Third Prince himself."

"But wasn't the young miss still under a betrothal arrangement? Does the Great Xia Dynasty not know?"

"What do you know about it? The Su Family's young master has been gone so many years. Who even knows if he's alive? And today, the young miss personally told the full story of his disappearance, right in front of the Su Family Patriarch, the City Lord, the Third Prince, and that senior from Moongaze Sect."

"Oh? What story? How have I never heard this?"

Su Yang's interest sharpened. He wanted to hear with his own ears exactly how this vicious woman had explained his disappearance.

"Well, you really can't blame our young miss. Apparently Young Master Su had trouble in a certain department. When he tried to share her chambers, he simply couldn't rise to the occasion. Too ashamed, so he left."

"Maybe he just couldn't face it. I mean, a man can fall short in plenty of things, but not that."

"What?! Ahahahaha! To think, the great Young Master Su had that kind of problem..."

"And our young miss publicly cast off Young Master Su right in front of the Su Family Patriarch, the City Lord, and the Moongaze Sect elder. Ha ha ha!"

The Wang Manor servants all burst into laughter by the stream.

What they did not know was that a pair of furious eyes were fixed on them from above, fists clenched tight, with the gaze of a starving predator locked on its prey.

---

[^1]: 妖孽 (yāo niè) — literally "demon-bastard" or "monstrous calamity," but in cultivation-novel idiom this is *high praise*: a prodigy whose talent is so extreme it borders on unnatural. The character is essentially complimenting Wang Yiyi by calling her a freak of nature. English has no clean equivalent — "monster" carries the right awe but reads negative; "prodigy" alone is too neutral.

[^2]: 关门弟子 (guān mén dì zi) — literally "closed-door disciple." The last student a master formally takes before "closing the door" on accepting any more — by tradition the most cherished, often the inheritor of the master's full transmission. For the Moongaze Sect Master to take Wang Yiyi as 关门弟子 is the highest possible mark of personal favor; she would inherit the sect master's deepest teachings.

[^3]: 装逼 (zhuāng bī) — crude modern Chinese slang for "pretentious posturing / showing off / trying to look cool." Cultivation novels lean on this term for comic-relief moments where a powerful being's vanity gets the better of them. Here the Lightning Leopard's worry is comically specific: it isn't afraid of losing the fight, it's afraid of looking uncool to potential mates. The crude register and the petty motivation are both part of the joke.
